PHYSICAL ACTIVITY

Cardio

Aerobic activities involve cardiovascular conditioning and is an outstanding and efficient way to burn calories. This low intensity excersise is generally practiced in order to increase heart health, lose weight and reduce stress. It is important to practice cardio excersise safely as repetitive movement over long durations of time can eventually lead to muscle and joint pain. A wide range of activities such as running, swimming, biking are classified as cardio and come with many benefits.

Stretching

Often times, stretching can improve flexibility and improve muscle pains and joint problems. Additionally, stretching can benifit ones mental health as well as physical health. Yoga is a practice that is accessible to a variety of levels – from beginners to competative athletes, the benefits of yoga can be enjoyed by all. Take a class at a local studio to try it out! Alternatively, there are an abundance of resources online that can be used to help one start out their practice at home.

Weights

Weight training is generally used to tone and strength one's body by creating lean muscle. Weightlifiting is a form of high intensity anaerobic conditioning. There are several benefits to weight training, some of which include: reduced cancer risk, improved flexibility and mobility, osteoporosis prevention and boosted mental health. Practicing weight training with the correct form can lead to a healthie lifestyle. Try it out at a local gym or at home with the guidance of online resources!

PHYSICAL & MENTAL HEALTH

Overview

The United States spends more per capita on healthcare than any other country, yet sees startling numbers of people suffer with heart disease, diabetes and cancer. According to The China Study, a comprehensive study linking animal product consumption to chronic illness, dietary changes can make significant positive impacts such as enabling diabetic patients to go off their medication and reversing heart disease.

Physical

According to the Office of Disease Prevention and Health Promotion, 1 in 3 adults and 1 in 6 adolescents are obese. The rate of obesity in young people in America has increased to such an extent that it has been predicted that this generation of children could be the first to live shorter and less healthy lives than their parents. According to the Harvard School of Public Health, limiting sugary and processed foods, limiting screen time and increasing physical activity are recommended ways to avoid such diseases. Even replacing one serving of meat a day with whole grains or healthy proteins was estimated to reduce diabetes risk by roughly 20 percent.

Mental

1 in 5 Americans experience mental illness and 1 in 5 youth ages 13-18 experience a severe mental disorder in their life, according to the National Alliance on Mental Illness. In order to lead a healthier lifestyle, it is essential to place emphasis on both mental and physical health. While taking care of your body is intertwined with taking care of your mind, steps should be taken to focus on the mental aspects. Small ways to make positive changes to your mental health include: surrounding yourself with positive people, volunteering to help others, spending time in nature, writing in a journal and practicing mindfulness and meditation, according to the University of Michigan. Connecting to resources and seeking help is important. (National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 1-800-273-TALK (8255).).

QUICK HEALTH TIPS

Here are some tips to help you on your physical health journey.

- Take yoga classes, go on runs, and workout with friends regularly to excersise while having fun!

- Stay Hydrated! To calculate how much water you should consume to stay hydrated and healthy.

Click below to check out how much water you should be taking in per day!

- Eat more vegetables and cut back on processed foods and added sugars to help your workouts to take full effect. Sugars make your body unable to reach its fruitful potential. See what I did there.

-If workouts are too intensive for you as you start out, try replacing them with stretches you enjoy instead and take frequent breaks. Include a mix of cardio and high intensity workouts in your weekly routines.

- Get into a comfortable sleep routine to help your body fall into a regular circadian rhythm.

- To boost brain power, eat a piece of dark chocolate or two for a fun way of gaining energy and focus!

- If you are feeling extra stressed, stop what you are doing, print out some adult coloring pages and color to calm yourself down and distract your mind.

- Looking at huge goals can be overwhelming. Instead break down these tasks into jobs that you are capable of completing a day.

- If you have trouble waking up early, each day, set your alarm 10-15 minutes earlier until you reach the ideal time to increase your energy and productivity for the day!

- Even though you might enjoy social media, take a break from it completely for a day, your mind will be reset and easier to understand.
